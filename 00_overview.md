# Film Restoration Pack for Old 8 mm Transfers

This pack summarizes accepted approaches for the main cleanup operations used on old 8 mm / Super 8 transfers.

## Core principle

Do not treat restoration as one giant filter. Separate the problems:

1. stabilize motion
2. correct flicker / exposure instability
3. remove dust / scratches / dirt
4. reduce noise or grain carefully
5. recover sharpness carefully
6. restore tone and color
7. upscale only after the image is otherwise under control

## Typical workflow order

A practical order for digital cleanup is:

1. source capture / best scan or transfer available
2. stabilization
3. deflicker
4. dust / scratch cleanup
5. denoise
6. deblur / sharpen
7. exposure and color correction
8. optional frame interpolation
9. optional upscale
10. final encode / archive master / delivery copy

## Important warnings

- Over-processing is the biggest failure mode.
- Stabilization and sharpening can make dust and flicker more obvious.
- Strong denoising often destroys film texture and facial detail.
- AI tools can hallucinate detail. Keep a conservative master.
- Keep the original scan or transfer untouched.
